# Company Earnings Report Comparison

We compared three models to see how well they analyze quarterly company earnings.

## Final Ranking

| Rank | Model | Score | Assessment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | quarterly-health-check (Opus) | 9.5/10 | Most accurate and professional |
| 2 | quarterly_analysis (Sonnet) | 8.8/10 | Strong professional review |
| 3 | quarterly_financial_analysis (Haiku) | 7.5/10 | Good structure but makes guesses |

---

## Why Opus Wins

### Key Finding
Opus was the only model to notice that the company is making less profit on its sales (flat profit despite rising revenue):

| Metric | Last Year | This Year |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Sales (Revenue) | 100 | 112 |
| Real Profit (Gross Profit) | 68 | 68.3 |

Even though sales grew by 12%, actual profit stayed almost the same. This is the most important finding in the report.

### Strong Financial Logic
Opus correctly points out:
- Costs are rising faster than sales.
- Profits are dropping.
- The company's total income is shrinking.
- Risks from high spending on AI systems.
- The difference between what management says and the real numbers.

### Great Questions
The model asks important questions:
- Are AI costs counted as basic production costs or daily running costs?
- Is AI spending a regular expense or a one-time long-term investment?
- When will profit margins start to recover?
- Is the company actually making money from AI yet?

---

## Sonnet Strengths
- Clear layout.
- Balanced points.
- Mentions risks well.
- *Note*: It missed the flat profit issue.

---

## Haiku Strengths and Limits
- Easy to read.
- Good quick summary.
- *Note*: It made guesses not supported by facts (like predicting future chances and using random benchmarks).

---

## Conclusion
For financial reviews, the models rank:
1. **Opus** (Best)
2. **Sonnet**
3. **Haiku**

Opus does the best job explaining the numbers and asking the right questions.
